Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Pandora

Is Pandora really secure?

expand_more

Yes, Pandora employs AES-256 encryption, the same standard used by governments and military organizations. Your data is encrypted locally before being sent to our servers, meaning only you can decrypt and access your information.

What happens if I forget my master password?

expand_more

Due to our zero-knowledge architecture, we cannot recover your master password if you forget it. This is intentional for maximum security. We recommend creating a password hint during setup and potentially storing a secure backup of your master password.

Can I access Pandora on multiple devices?

expand_more

Absolutely! Pandora syncronizes your encrypted data across all your devices, allowing you to access your passwords and personal information securely from anywhere.

Is there a limit to how many passwords I can store?

expand_more

No, there's no limit to the number of passwords, notes, or personal information entries you can store with Pandora, regardless of which plan you're on.

How does the categorization system work?

expand_more

Pandora allows you to create custom categories to organize your passwords and personal data. You can create categories for work, personal, financial, or any other grouping that makes sense for your needs.

Is my data backed up automatically?

expand_more

Yes, your encrypted data is automatically backed up to our secure servers. This ensures that even if you lose your device, your important information remains safe and accessible once you log in again.
